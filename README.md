# China vs USA — 3D Printer & Filament Price Comparison

I've spent years working closely with the Chinese 3D printing market. One thing that keeps coming up when I talk to makers in the US: they have no idea what this stuff actually costs in China.

Not because they don't care — because the information just isn't out there in English.

So I started tracking it myself.

---

## What this is

A simple, regularly updated comparison of what 3D printers and filament cost on Chinese platforms (JD.com, Taobao) versus what you pay in the US (Amazon, official stores).

That's it. No fluff. Just numbers.

---

## Why it matters

These machines are made in China. The filament is made in China. But by the time they reach an Amazon warehouse, some of them cost twice as much.

If you're a hobbyist trying to figure out whether to buy a second printer, or a small business watching material costs eat into margins, knowing the actual source price helps you make better decisions.

And honestly — 3D printing should be more accessible than it is. Printing a replacement part for a broken kitchen tool, making something with your kids, running a small custom figurine business from home — none of that should require a big budget. The hardware is cheap at the source. The gap is a information problem as much as a tariff problem.

---

## Where we're headed

Right now this is manual — I check prices monthly and update the files.

Eventually I'd like to use AI tools to automate the data collection and pull in more from Chinese maker communities: new model launches, material reviews, price drops. There's a ton of useful knowledge being shared in Chinese that never makes it to English forums.

If you want to help with that part, open an issue or reach out.

## How prices are collected

- China: JD.com official brand stores
- USA: Amazon + official brand US stores
- Exchange rate: updated monthly (current: 1 USD ≈ 7.25 CNY)
- Regular prices only — no coupons or flash sales
